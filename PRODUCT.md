# Product

## Register

brand

## Platform

web

## Users

KLyric's website serves KDE Plasma 6 users who listen through Cider and want synchronized lyrics visible in their desktop panel. Visitors need to understand the utility quickly, confirm that it fits their Linux setup and privacy expectations, and reach the release or installation instructions without friction.

## Product Purpose

The site presents KLyric as a focused open-source Linux utility: a Cider plugin reads the active synchronized line, a loopback-only authenticated bridge keeps the current state in memory, and a Plasma widget renders that line in the panel. Success means a qualified visitor understands that flow in about ten seconds and can confidently download v0.1.0, read the installation guide, or inspect the source.

## Positioning

KLyric puts Cider's active synchronized lyric directly in a KDE Plasma 6 panel while keeping playback and lyric state local to the user's machine.

## Conversion & proof

- Primary CTA: download the latest release.
- Secondary CTA: view the GitHub repository; the installation guide is the supporting action.
- The line a visitor remembers after ten seconds: synchronized lyrics, right in your Plasma panel.
- Belief ladder: KLyric solves a clear desktop need; it fits Cider and Plasma 6; communication is local-only; the project is transparent and open source; installation guidance and a release are available.
- Proof on hand: the production architecture, v0.1.0 release, MIT license, repository documentation, and an interface preview based on the shipped widget behavior.

## Brand Personality

Precise, mechanical, and restrained. The interface should feel like a compact terminal utility belonging to the same visual family as luizpaes.dev: direct, technically credible, and calm rather than promotional.

## Anti-references

The site must not resemble a generic SaaS template. Avoid glassmorphism, decorative shadows, gradients, oversized pills, stock photography, floating cards, purple AI styling, excessive animation, remote asset dependencies, and unsupported product claims.

## Design Principles

- Explain the product through its real architecture and interface, not abstract marketing language.
- Make the privacy boundary visible: loopback-only communication, in-memory state, and no lyric uploads.
- Keep the page compact enough to scan in one desktop viewport while preserving every meaningful detail on smaller screens.
- Let terminal framing, exact typography, and one cyan accent carry the identity.
- Make all primary paths obvious, accessible, and useful without JavaScript.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG 2.2 AA where reasonably possible. Use semantic landmarks and headings, visible keyboard focus, readable contrast, touch targets of at least 44 by 44 pixels where appropriate, reduced-motion support, robust narrow-screen wrapping, and text that does not depend on color alone.
